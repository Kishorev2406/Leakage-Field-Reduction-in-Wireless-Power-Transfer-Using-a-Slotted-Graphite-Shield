Wireless Power Transfer (WPT) is an emerging technology that enables the transmission of electrical energy without the use of physical wires. However, conventional WPT systems suffer from problems such as leakage magnetic fields, eddy current losses, and reduced efficiency, which limit their performance and practical implementation. Traditional shielding materials such as aluminium and ferrite are commonly used to reduce magnetic field leakage, but aluminium produces high eddy current losses while ferrite is expensive and brittle. Therefore, there is a need for an improved shielding technique that can effectively reduce losses and enhance power transfer efficiency.

This project proposes the design and performance analysis of a slotted graphite shield for wireless power transfer systems. The proposed system consists of transmitter and receiver coils operating at a resonant frequency of 50 kHz, with slotted graphite shielding placed on the transmitter and receiver sides. The slot structure interrupts circulating eddy current paths within the shield, thereby reducing power losses and improving magnetic field confinement. Graphite is selected due to its moderate conductivity, lightweight nature, and cost-effectiveness compared to conventional shielding materials.

The system is designed and analyzed using ANSYS Maxwell, where parameters such as leakage magnetic field, ohmic loss, solid loss, induced voltage, and inductance are evaluated. The performance of the proposed slotted graphite shield is compared with conventional aluminium and solid graphite shields. Simulation results demonstrate that the slotted graphite shielding significantly reduces magnetic field leakage and losses while increasing induced voltage and overall power transfer efficiency.

The proposed design provides a reliable, efficient, and economical solution for modern wireless power transfer applications. It can be effectively used in electric vehicle charging systems, consumer electronics, biomedical devices, industrial automation, and other wireless energy transfer applications where high efficiency and reduced electromagnetic interference are required.
